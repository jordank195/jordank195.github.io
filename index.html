Apple has announced a halt in the sales of its Series 9 and Ultra 2 smartwatches in the United States due to a patent dispute related to the blood oxygen feature technology. The U.S. International Trade Commission (ITC) issued an order in October that could potentially prevent Apple from importing its watches, citing a violation of medical technology company Masimo's patent rights.

The decision is under review by President Joe Biden until December 25, and if not vetoed, the sales ban would be effective from December 26. Apple intends to comply with the ruling while also expressing disagreement and planning to appeal.

Scientists have crafted VR goggles for mice, giving them a taste of the "wild" life – complete with the thrill of being pursued by a bird. Meet the iMRSIV, or the Miniature Rodent Stereo Illumination VR. Forget headsets wrapping around their heads; these specs perch in front of mouse faces, offering a 3D escapade. The mice, unable to see the real world, either dash faster on their tiny treadmills or freeze in response to the simulated avian menace.

The researchers at Northwestern University hope to delve deeper into mouse behavior, possibly even staging scenarios where the mouse becomes the hunter, chasing flies in the world of mouse VR. The mice, rocking their VR gear, displayed brain activity akin to their free-roaming counterparts, proving that mouse brains know how to party in the virtual realm.

The hope? These budget-friendly mouse goggles might unlock secrets of the human brain's reaction to repeated VR exposure, all without the need for extravagant lab setups. Watch out, rodent gamers – the future is now!
