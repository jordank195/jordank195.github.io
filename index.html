This week, Spotify dropped Spotify Wrapped. Oh and Apple did their thing too. Not biased, I promise.

If you don‚Äôt know what that is, let me help you out. Over the course of the year, Spotify tracks your listening habits, and at the end of the year it lets you know your top songs, top artists and whatnot. It‚Äôs allegedly an idea that an intern at Spotify came up with, so props to them. I was pleasantly unsurprised to find that Everything Everything is my top artist. It‚Äôs unsurprising, first because I listen to a lot of Everything Everything ‚Äì top 0.05% thank you very much ‚Äì and also because a friend of mine recently introduced me to a very cool app called stats.fm. You link it with your Spotify account, and then you can track your listening habits yourself. It will give you your top artists and songs over any time period, and you can go back and look at your stats from like 2015. It also tells you when you first listened to an artist. After a four-year wait, Tesla's Cybertruck has been delivered to its first customers in an event at the company's Texas headquarters. Elon Musk announced updated details on pricing, range, and features. The rear-wheel drive version starts at $60,990 with 250 miles of range, while the dual-motor and tri-motor versions are set for 2024 release, priced at $79,990 and $99,990, respectively. The event featured a redo of the infamous window strength demonstration from 2019, with improved results. (The window didn‚Äôt break this time)

The Tesla Cybertruck boasts a distinctive and polarising design with its angular, polygonal shape and sharp stainless steel exterior. The unconventional aesthetic, characterised by its futuristic and robust appearance, signals Tesla's departure from traditional truck designs and aims to set the Cybertruck apart in the market. Despite challenges in production and a polarising design, Tesla aims to produce 250,000 trucks annually by 2025. The Cybertruck's impact in the competitive truck market remains uncertain.

Oh and if you were hoping for one this side of the pond, good luck. In its current form, the Cybertruck will not pass the stringent safety requirements for sale in the EU, and Tesla have no plans to sell it in the UK either.
