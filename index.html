Towards the end of last month, Indian space agency Isro successfully put their first lander, Vikram, on the Moon. Vikram is the lander module of Chandrayaan-3 and comes paired with the Pragyan rover (Pragyan actually travelled in Vikram's belly).

 

Before we continue, I need to explain Lunar Day and Lunar Night. A lunar day is the time it takes for the moon to complete one rotation on its axis, lasting about 29.5 days. The moon is tidally locked to the Earth, so one side always faces the Earth while the other side remains hidden from view. This phenomenon is due to the moon's orbit around the earth, which occurs as both the earth and the moon orbit the sun. In contrast, an Earth day is 24 hours long, with 12 hours of daylight and 12 hours of nighttime, determined by the Earth's rotation on its axis. The lunar day, approximately 14 days in length, results in 14 days of sunlight on one side of the moon and 14 days of darkness on the other side.

 

Still following? Good. 

 

Now, here's where it gets amusing. As the Sun starts to set on Vikram and Pragyan's lunar abode, Isro is tucking them into a lunar slumber. Isro says they'll "fall asleep next to each other." So, while you're crawling into bed solo tonight, just remember that two robots a quarter of a million miles away have a more happening bedtime story than you do.

 

Sweet dreams, lunar buddies! 🌕🤖😴

It's that time of year again when a load of tech companies announce new stuff. Things kick off next Tuesday with Apple's September keynote, where they'll announce the iPhone 15 line, new Apple Watches, and potentially new Airpods.

 

The star of Tuesday's show will be the USB-C port. But don't let Apple fool you into thinking this is out of the kindness of their hearts. The EU recently passed legislation forcing tech manufacturers to use a common port on their devices, forcing Apple's hand.

 

If you're wondering why Apple would be so reluctant to ditch the lightning port, they charge fees for any product that uses that port (and wants to actually work with your phone). Any 3rd party charger, dock, battery case or whatever that uses the lightning port generates a little kickback for Apple. And that kickback stacked up, to the tune of $5 billion a year.  So yeah, you can bet Apple isn't too pleased about the situation internally.
