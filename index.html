A word from Sarah Akande

Deputy Head of Access

Hey guys!

My name is Sarah Akande and I am a second-year English student at the home of Cambridge’s most gorgeous red brick, best proximity to the Sidgwick Site for all those last-minute rushes to lectures, and (overpriced but) tasty brunch: Selwyn College!!

I’m usually seen around Cambridge doing anything and everything but studying, included but not limited to: a lot of social justice work, graphic design, Christian Union shenanigans, dabbling in sports and fitness, rinsing my bank account in the theatre and occasionally opening a book or two!

Oh, and I neglected to mention the best society of them all: Cam FM!! I host a show called Melanin Queens, designed to uplift Cambridge’s lovely women of colour, and also occasionally can be heard on Her Voice. I’m also Cam FM’s Diversity Officer so am really committed to getting as many people of all backgrounds onto the radio – we need everyone’s voices! Stay tuned for news about how Cam FM will be celebrating Black History Month on the weekend of Saturday 28th to Sunday 29th – it’d be incredible to have all those of Afro-Caribbean heritage sign-up to be on-air then. Super excited for all that Cam FM will get up to in this new Michaelmas Term and looking forward to meeting more of you guys at events!

Cam FM News!

Hi all, Aaron here, Cam FM’s Head of News this year. Cam FM is a great opportunity to get involved in student journalism in a friendly, low-commitment capacity. With our weekly news show, Cam FM News Brunch, we cover a range of local and student stories, and we’d love to have you on board to pursue a story which interests you, which we can then get to air. There is no pressure to submit a stream of stories at regular intervals – we encourage engaging with Cam FM News as and when you feel like it.

The possibilities for stories you could cover are endless, from the effects of prolonged industrial action such as the MAB to a story about a local show or gig that you may have seen. Overall, it’s a great way to get your voice on the radio in a timeframe that works for you.

If you’re a Cam FM member who has passed their assessment and this sounds interesting, drop us a line at news@camfm.co.uk – we’d love to hear from you üòä

Possibly the best news all week: this weekend is the World Conker Championships in Southwick, Northamptonshire.

For those unaware, conkers are the brown seeds from a Horse Chestnut tree. They are oblong-shaped, with a hard brown shell. In the game of conkers, you tie a bit of string through your conker and try to smash your opponent’s conker with your own. The winner is the player without a smashed conker.

The current men’s and women’s world champion are Randy Topolnitsky and Fee Aylmore.
