‘Ello ‘ello ‘ello!

My name’s Alex, and I’m Cam FM’s current Deputy Head of Music. Interesting tidbits

I imagine many of you will have at some point listened to "The Ketchup Song" (Aserejé by Spanish girl group Las Ketchup). Here's a reminder in case you've forgotten.

   

I recently learned what the song is actually about! It's centred around a fella called Diego, who they've just met in a nightclub. Diego reeeally wants the DJ to play his favourite song. He can't quite remember what that song is called, but he says it goes like this:

  

"Aserejé, ja, de jé, de jebe tu de jebere sebiunouva majabi an de bugui an de buididipí..."

  

This is actually total jibberish (in both Spanish and English) until you realise that it is actually Diego phonetically singing none other than Rapper's Delight by The Sugarhill Gang!

 

"I said a hip hop the hippie the hippie to the hip hip hop, a you don't stop the rock it to the bang bang boogie say up jumped the boogie to the rhythm of the boogie, the beat..."

  

I'll admit I did go a little crazy when I was first told this - but mostly because I performed Rapper's Delight in a band in Year 11. I don't really fancy linking that (but I'll tease you by saying it's somewhere on YouTube), so instead, enjoy the original:

A turtle from 4,000 miles away that washed up on a beach in Flintshire, Wales has been returned home. The turtle is a Kemp's ridley turtle, one of the most endangered species worldwide, and is mainly found in the Gulf of Mexico and off eastern North America.

  

After beaching in 2021, it was nursed back to health by Anglesey Sea Zoo, and was flown back to Texas, where it is continuing care at Houston Zoo.

 ARM is a Cambridge-based semiconductor design company that is behind base designs of the processors in your phones/tablets/(some) laptops and much more. Last week, it completed its Initial Public Offering (IPO) in New York, where it offered up shares in the company to the public, allowing individuals and institutional investors to buy ownership stakes in the company. ARM's listing opened at $51 per share, valuing it at over $54 billion and making this IPO one of the largest since 2021 in New York .
